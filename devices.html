2015

For my birthday I got my hands on a used iPhone 5 from Craigslist, my dad never believed in buying new electronics. It was the first time I owned a device that was truly mine and had free access to the internet. I found Instagram and started running accounts around my interests, one of them being a hockey account. This is where I began getting into graphic design.

2017

My friends and I played hockey in my garage every week, and one day I stole my dad's camera and started filming. That's how "NHL Production" was born, not exactly NHL level, but it's where I learned to edit using cracked Final Cut, Google Slides, and a pirated Photoshop just to make it work.

2018

On a hot summer day, deep in my Instagram explore page, I stumbled onto a style of video editing that felt fluid and alive and I knew I had to try it. I jumped into After Effects, made edits of my favorite rappers, and built a following of 6k before I burned out. It felt like wasted time at the time, but little did I know this would eventually lead me to finding my career path.

2020

Covid turned my days into a loop of gaming, eating, and sleeping with Fortnite being the only thing keeping me sane. When Fortnite montage edits started blowing up, I taught myself Premiere Pro overnight just to make my own. This is also when I started rocking the #0000FF profile picture, which became a part of my brand.

2021

I enrolled in Computer Science at SFU thinking I'd be building apps, not realizing what I actually wanted was to design them. A friend mentioned IAT 100 used After Effects, and because of all the time I put into it back in 2018, I knew I would enjoy it so I took it and fell in love. I switched to Interactive Arts and Technology and never looked back.

2022

On my 22nd birthday, I committed to vlogging my life for 365 days, using a mini design project as the anchor for each video. It only lasted a month as a vlog, but I kept designing for the entire year. Looking back at where I started versus where I finished is one of the most satisfying things I've ever felt.
