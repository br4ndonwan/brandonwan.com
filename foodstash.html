OVERVIEW

The Work Was Real. The Website Wasn't Ready

Food Stash is a non-profit organization that rescues surplus food and redistributes it to communities facing food insecurity.

Despite a strong mission and active programs, Food Stash's website was not structured to convert visitors into engaged supporters.

PROBLEM

3 Friction Points Holding Back Conversion

Food Stash's website had a lot to offer but wasn't built to show it. Three core friction points were standing between visitors and action.

Confusing Navigation and CTA's
Key pathways like programs and donations lacked clear prioritization, making action harder to find.
Inefficient Booking Flow
Rentals and events relied on back-and-forth emails, lacking a streamlined system for conversion.
Low Content Engagement
Dense, static layouts made exploration feel passive rather than guided.

SOLUTION

Food Stash, Redesigned

A website rebuilt from the ground up to turn visitors into supporters.

CORE FLOWS

Redesigned Navigation

A navigation bar that tells visitors exactly where to go and what to expect.

Interactive Rental System

A self-serve booking experience that replaces back and forth emails entirely.

Visual Identity Refresh

Micro-interactions, custom illustrations, and a refined color system that brings the mission to life.

RESEARCH

Understanding The Space

We researched Food Stash's mission, programs, and positioning, while looking at how comparable organizations present themselves online.

Breaking Down The Existing Experience

We conducted a website audit and built a sitemap to visualize every page and interaction. Three issues surfaced immediately:

  • No clear sense of where to go or what to do
  • Content was overwhelming and hard to skim
  • Booking relied entirely on back-and-forth emails

From there, we shaped our interview questions around the gaps we had already identified.

DESIGN DECISION

An Interactive Map To Sell The Space

The stakeholder flagged that renters weren't getting enough context to commit. Rather than adding more static content, I designed an interactive map where clients can explore the space, click into individual rooms, and see details in context.

A Nav That Explains Itself

Food Stash's programs aren't self-explanatory to a first-time visitor. I redesigned the nav to include a brief description for each page and supporting icons to aid recognition.

Micro-Interactions To Build Identity

To address the lack of identity, I introduced hover animations on CTAs, loading animations between states, and custom illustrations built in collaboration with my teammate.

TAKEAWAYS

Process Over Ego

A 3-day hackathon teaches you fast. With a tight timeline and a team, I learned that strong task distribution matters more than individual output. Dropping your ego and trusting your teammates is what actually moves the work forward. Setting both soft and hard deadlines kept us accountable and made sure nothing fell through the cracks at the end.
