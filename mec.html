OVERVIEW

MEC built their identity on community, then lost it. How do we bring it back?

MEC started as a Canadian co-operative where members were also co-owners, but in 2020 the board sold to a U.S. private equity firm, fracturing the trust that defined the brand for 50 years.

In 2025, MEC returned to Canadian ownership and is now working to rebuild that connection with its community.

PROBLEM

They're Canada's leading outdoor retailer...

...but did you know they host community events? Neither did 97% of the customers we interviewed.

Events have always been a pillar of MEC's identity, yet they're nearly impossible to find and even harder to join.

SOLUTION

MEC Events: a dedicated app for discovering and joining MEC community events.

Discover Events

Filter by location, skill level, and activity type to find events that fit.

Event Details

Browse events by location, skill level, and activity type

Community Spaces

Connect with other attendees before the event even starts.

Rewards

Earn badges and unlock coupons through event participation

INITIAL RESEARCH

We went to the store to find the problem

Our brief was open-ended, so we ran four research activities to identify where to focus.

Field Observation
Conducted an ethnography at the store observing signage, displays, and customer behavior.
Interviews
Spoke with staff, customers, and stakeholders to understand their relationship with MEC.
Secondary Research
Analyzed reviews, Reddit threads, and forums to surface broader sentiment and recurring frustrations.

MEC has a community problem, not a product problem

After conducting a thematic analysis across our field observations, interviews, and secondary research, three recurring themes emerged that shaped our direction.

Fragmented discovery
Event information lived across the website, Instagram, and third-party platforms, creating an inconsistent experience with no single place to find reliable details.
Lack of Clarity
Key details like required gear, skill level, and host expectations were often missing, increasing hesitation and lowering confidence before signing up.
Disconnected from Community
Events had no tie to membership benefits or community features, giving members no reason to return and leaving MEC with no way to track engagement.

To understand where the experience broke down, we mapped the current user journey from discovering an event to signing up and attending.

It wasn't just hard to find, it was hard to care about

Using Jesse, a persona we created to represent beginner MEC customers, we mapped the end-to-end event experience.

Key insight: Even when members stumbled across an event, the lack of detail, community, and follow-through gave them little reason to sign up or come back.

CO-DESIGN WORKSHOP

We held a participatory workshop with MEC stakeholders, which pushed us to reframe our approach to consider both community value and business impact.

Key insight Events needed to support repeat attendance and spending, not just first-time discovery. This is what introduced the idea of a badge and progression system.

BUSINESS REFRAME

Community value and business impact are not mutually exclusive

A badge and rewards system would motivate members to return while giving MEC the data to justify investing more in events.

IDEATION

Improve the website event page
Redesign the existing events section to be easier to find and navigate.
Physical event card with QR code
Hand out cards in-store that members can scan to explore upcoming events.

Neither solved the full problem

Improving the website still leaves events buried within a larger retail experience. A physical card increases visibility but does nothing to improve the experience once someone gets there. Neither option addressed the lack of community, clarity, or reason to return.

Discover — events needed to be front and center, not buried. The home screen immediately surfaces upcoming events. Filter by skill level, activity, and date so beginners like Jesse don't feel overwhelmed.

Event details — members were signing up without knowing what to expect. The details page removes hesitation with an agenda, what to bring, and highlights from past sessions.

Community spaces — first-timers like Jesse were nervous about showing up alone. Post-signup, every event has a community tab for announcements and discussion so attendees can connect before the day arrives.

Badges and rewards — came from the workshop insight that events needed a reason to come back. Attend 5 events, get 10% off. Attend 10, get 20% off. This also gives MEC trackable engagement data to justify investing more in events.
